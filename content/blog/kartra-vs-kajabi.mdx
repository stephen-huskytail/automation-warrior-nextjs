If you sell online courses, coaching programs, or memberships, you have probably narrowed your platform shortlist to two names: Kartra and Kajabi.

Both promise an all-in-one platform that replaces a half-dozen separate tools. Both handle course hosting, email marketing, landing pages, and payments. And both charge enough per month that you want to pick the right one the first time.

I have built funnels, email sequences, and membership areas inside both platforms. This comparison is based on that hands-on experience — not a feature matrix I pulled from a sales page.

>
> - Kartra wins on funnel building, checkout flexibility, and marketing automation depth
> - Kajabi wins on course creation tools, user experience, and community features

> - Choose Kartra if your business runs on funnels and email sequences
> - Choose Kajabi if your primary product is courses and you want the cleanest learner experience

## What Kartra and Kajabi Actually Do

Both are cloud-based platforms built for knowledge entrepreneurs — course creators, coaches, consultants, and info product sellers. They bundle tools that would otherwise require separate subscriptions.

Kartra launched in 2018 and leans heavily into marketing automation. It was built by the team behind WebinarJam and EverWebinar, so the DNA is direct response marketing.

Kajabi launched in 2010 and started as a pure course platform. It has expanded into email, pages, and community over the years, but the course experience remains the center of gravity.

If you want a deeper look at either platform individually, check the full [Kartra review](/blog/kartra-review) or the [Kartra vs GoHighLevel comparison](/blog/kartra-vs-gohighlevel) for a different angle.

## Course Creation and Content Delivery

This is where Kajabi pulls ahead.

Kajabi's course builder is genuinely excellent. You get multiple content types (video, text, downloads, quizzes), drip scheduling, progress tracking, completion certificates, and a clean student dashboard that looks professional without custom design work.

Kartra has a membership and course area, but it feels like it was bolted on after the marketing engine was built — because it was. You can deliver content, gate it behind payment, and drip it over time. But the student experience is more utilitarian. The interface works, but it does not feel as polished as Kajabi's.

### Community Features

Kajabi added a built-in community feature that works like a lightweight Circle or Skool alternative. Students can post, comment, and interact inside your Kajabi site without leaving the platform.

Kartra does not have native community. You would need to connect a third-party community tool, which adds complexity and cost.

**Winner: Kajabi** — the course builder and community features are meaningfully better.

## Funnel Building and Landing Pages

This is where Kartra takes the lead.

Kartra's page and funnel builder is one of the most capable drag-and-drop builders on the market. You get behavioral triggers, exit-intent popups, countdown timers, A/B testing, and multi-step funnels with conditional logic. The template library is extensive, and the builder gives you granular control over layout without needing code.

Kajabi's page builder is functional but more limited. You work within pre-designed sections and have less flexibility on layout customization. It is faster to get a clean page live, but harder to build complex multi-step funnels with conditional branching.

If your business model depends on webinar funnels, tripwire sequences, or multi-step launch funnels, Kartra gives you significantly more firepower.

**Winner: Kartra** — deeper funnel logic, better A/B testing, more design flexibility.

## Email Marketing and Automation

Both platforms include email marketing. The difference is depth.

Kartra's automation builder uses a visual flowchart-style interface where you can branch based on behavior: page visits, video watch percentage, email opens, link clicks, tag assignments, purchase history, and more. You can build genuinely complex sequences that rival dedicated email platforms like ActiveCampaign.

Kajabi's email tool handles broadcasts and basic automations well. You can send emails based on purchases, form submissions, and a few behavioral triggers. But the automation logic is simpler — fewer branching conditions, fewer trigger types, less granular segmentation.

For a solo course creator sending weekly newsletters and a welcome sequence, Kajabi is perfectly fine. For a business that runs multi-step nurture campaigns with behavioral branching, Kartra is the stronger choice.

**Winner: Kartra** — more automation depth, more trigger types, more flexibility.

## Checkout and Payments

Both platforms process payments through Stripe (and Kartra also supports PayPal natively).

Kartra's checkout is more feature-rich. You get order bumps, one-click upsells, downsells, payment plans, trial periods, coupon codes, and built-in affiliate tracking on every checkout. The checkout pages themselves are customizable within the page builder.

Kajabi handles payments cleanly with support for one-time payments, subscriptions, payment plans, and free trials. It recently added order bumps. But it lacks the full upsell/downsell funnel flow that Kartra offers out of the box.

If you are running a simple course checkout, both work fine. If you are optimizing average order value with upsell sequences, Kartra is built for that.

**Winner: Kartra** — more checkout options, native upsells and downsells.

## Ease of Use

Kajabi wins on simplicity. The interface is clean, modern, and intuitive. Most creators can build a course, connect a payment, and launch a basic funnel within a day. The learning curve is gentle.

Kartra has more features, which means more complexity. The dashboard can feel overwhelming at first. The page builder is powerful but has a steeper learning curve. Once you learn it, you move fast — but the first week or two requires more patience.

If you are not particularly technical and want to focus on creating content rather than configuring marketing systems, Kajabi will feel more comfortable.

**Winner: Kajabi** — cleaner interface, faster time to first launch.

## Integrations and Ecosystem

Neither platform is as open as a dedicated automation tool like [Make.com or Zapier](/blog/make-vs-zapier), but both connect to the major services.

Kajabi integrates natively with Stripe, Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, and a handful of email tools. It has a Zapier integration for connecting to other apps, but native integrations are limited.

Kartra has more built-in integrations: payment gateways (Stripe, PayPal, Authorize.net, Braintree), email services, membership platforms, and calendaring tools. It also has its own API and a Zapier connection.

For businesses that need deep workflow automation beyond what either platform offers natively, connecting to an external automation layer like n8n or Make.com is the practical path. The [business automation guide](/blog/business-automation-guide) covers how to think about that stack.

## My Honest Take

I have recommended both platforms to different clients based on their situations. The honest answer is that neither is universally "better" — they optimize for different things.

If I am talking to someone whose business is fundamentally about teaching — courses, memberships, coaching — and they want the cleanest possible experience for their students, I point them to Kajabi.

If I am talking to someone whose business is built on marketing funnels, email sequences, and maximizing revenue per customer, I point them to Kartra. The automation engine and checkout flexibility are genuinely powerful.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can I migrate from Kajabi to Kartra or vice versa?

Yes, but it is manual work. Neither platform offers a one-click migration tool. You would need to export your contacts (CSV), rebuild your pages and funnels in the new platform, and re-upload your course content. Email sequences need to be recreated from scratch. Budget a few days for the migration depending on how much content you have.

### Does Kajabi or Kartra handle SEO better?

Both are adequate for basic SEO — you can set page titles, meta descriptions, and URL slugs. Neither is a WordPress-level SEO platform. Kajabi has slightly cleaner default URL structures. For serious SEO, you would want a dedicated content site regardless of which platform you use for course delivery.

### Can I use Kartra or Kajabi with GoHighLevel?

Yes. Many businesses use GoHighLevel as their CRM and client communication hub while using Kartra or Kajabi specifically for course delivery. You can connect them via Zapier or webhooks to sync contacts and trigger automations across platforms. The [GoHighLevel CRM tutorial](/blog/gohighlevel-crm-tutorial) walks through the CRM side of that setup.

### Which platform has better customer support?

Both offer email and chat support. Kajabi is generally faster on response times and has a more active user community. Kartra's support is competent but can be slower during peak periods. Both have extensive knowledge bases and video tutorials.
