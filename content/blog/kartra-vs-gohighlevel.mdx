If you're shopping for an all-in-one marketing platform in 2026, two names keep surfacing: **Kartra** and **GoHighLevel**. Both promise to replace your patchwork of tools with a single dashboard — but they're built for very different users. In this head-to-head comparison, I'll break down pricing, features, automations, and real-world use cases so you can pick the right platform without wasting months on the wrong one.

> **Quick Summary**
> - **Kartra** excels at course creation, checkout pages, and built-in video hosting for solopreneurs and course creators.
> - **GoHighLevel** dominates for agencies and local businesses with unlimited sub-accounts, white-labeling, and two-way SMS/call automation.
> - GoHighLevel starts at **$97/month**; Kartra starts at **$119/month** — but GHL includes features Kartra charges add-ons for.
> - If you run an agency or manage multiple clients, GoHighLevel is the clear winner.
> - If you sell courses or digital products as a solopreneur, Kartra's native tools are hard to beat.

## What Is Kartra?

Kartra is a cloud-based marketing platform that bundles landing pages, email marketing, checkout pages, membership sites, video hosting, and helpdesk ticketing into one interface. It launched in 2018 under the Genesis Digital umbrella and has steadily built a loyal following among course creators, coaches, and info-product sellers.

Kartra's strength is its **all-in-one simplicity** — you can build a funnel, sell a course, onboard students, and handle support tickets without leaving the platform. If you've read our [Kartra review](/blog/kartra-review), you already know the platform delivers a polished experience for digital product businesses.

## What Is GoHighLevel?

GoHighLevel (GHL) is a CRM and marketing automation platform built specifically for **agencies, consultants, and local service businesses**. It launched in 2018 and has grown explosively — largely because of its unlimited sub-account model that lets agencies manage dozens (or hundreds) of clients from a single subscription.

GHL includes a CRM, funnel builder, email and SMS marketing, appointment booking, reputation management, pipeline tracking, AI-powered voice and chat agents, and a full white-label SaaS mode. If you want the full breakdown, read our [GoHighLevel review](/blog/go-high-level-review) or start with [What Is GoHighLevel?](/blog/what-is-gohighlevel).

## Feature-by-Feature Breakdown

### CRM and Pipeline Management

**GoHighLevel** was built as a CRM first. Its pipeline management is visual, customizable, and deeply integrated with SMS, calls, and email sequences. You can track every lead from first touch to closed deal with automations firing at each stage.

**Kartra** has a basic lead management system with tags and scoring, but it doesn't offer true pipeline visualization. If you need deal tracking and sales stage management, GHL is in a different league. For a deep dive on GHL's CRM, check our [GoHighLevel CRM tutorial](/blog/gohighlevel-crm-tutorial) — oh wait, that's coming soon. In the meantime, our [GoHighLevel automation workflows guide](/blog/gohighlevel-automation-workflows) shows how GHL's CRM and automations work together.

**Winner: GoHighLevel**

### Funnel and Page Builder

Both platforms include drag-and-drop funnel builders, and honestly, both get the job done.

**Kartra's** builder is more polished out of the box — cleaner templates, smoother checkout flows, and tighter integration with its membership and video hosting tools. If your entire business runs through funnels (webinar → offer → checkout → membership), Kartra's builder feels purpose-built for that workflow.

**GoHighLevel's** funnel builder is solid and improving rapidly, but it's more utilitarian. It prioritizes flexibility over visual polish. For agencies building client funnels at scale, GHL's templates and quick-clone functionality are faster to deploy.

**Winner: Kartra** (for design polish) | **GoHighLevel** (for agency-scale deployment)

### Email and SMS Marketing

**GoHighLevel** offers both email and two-way SMS natively. You can build entire nurture sequences that alternate between email, text messages, voicemail drops, and even AI-powered calls. This multi-channel approach is a massive advantage for local businesses where text response rates dwarf email open rates.

**Kartra** handles email marketing well — behavioral triggers, sequences, broadcasts, A/B testing — but SMS is limited. There's no native two-way texting or voice automation, which means you'd need a third-party tool like Twilio to fill the gap.

**Winner: GoHighLevel**

### Course Creation and Memberships

This is Kartra's home turf. The platform includes a full membership portal with drip content scheduling, progress tracking, quizzes, and built-in video hosting (no Vimeo or Wistia subscription needed). If you sell online courses, coaching programs, or paid communities, Kartra handles it all natively.

GoHighLevel added memberships and course hosting, but the feature set is still maturing. It works for basic course delivery, but lacks the polish and depth that dedicated course creators expect.

**Winner: Kartra**

### White-Labeling and Agency Features

This is GoHighLevel's defining advantage. The SaaS Pro plan lets you **white-label the entire platform** — your branding, your domain, your pricing — and resell it as your own software. You can build a recurring SaaS revenue stream on top of GHL's infrastructure without writing a line of code.

Kartra has no white-label option. It's built for end users, not resellers.

For agencies evaluating this angle, our guides on [GoHighLevel SaaS mode](/blog/gohighlevel-saas-mode) and [GoHighLevel for agencies](/blog/gohighlevel-for-agencies) break down exactly how the model works.

**Winner: GoHighLevel** (no contest)

### Automation Workflows

Both platforms offer visual automation builders, but the depth differs significantly.

**GoHighLevel's** workflow builder supports complex branching logic with triggers from SMS, calls, form submissions, appointment bookings, pipeline stage changes, and AI agent handoffs. You can build automations that respond to a missed call with an SMS, trigger a voicemail drop, and escalate to a human — all in one workflow.

**Kartra's** automation builder (called "Sequences") is powerful for email-centric workflows. Behavioral triggers, if/then branching, and tag-based logic are all well-implemented. But the trigger universe is smaller — it's primarily tied to email opens, clicks, page visits, and purchases.

**Winner: GoHighLevel** (broader trigger set and multi-channel execution)

### AI Features

GoHighLevel has invested heavily in AI over the past year. The platform now includes [AI agents](/blog/gohighlevel-ai-agents) for chat and voice, AI-powered conversation summaries, and smart appointment booking bots. These features are particularly impactful for local businesses that lose leads to slow response times.

Kartra has added some AI-assisted copywriting and content tools, but doesn't offer autonomous AI agents that can handle inbound calls or qualify leads independently.

**Winner: GoHighLevel**

## My Recommendation

After working with both platforms across multiple client accounts, here's my honest take: **GoHighLevel delivers more value per dollar for most businesses** — especially agencies and service providers who need CRM depth, multi-channel communication, and scalable client management.

Kartra still earns its spot for **dedicated course creators and info-product sellers** who want a self-contained ecosystem without juggling integrations. The membership tools, native video hosting, and checkout experience are genuinely best-in-class for that use case.

But if you're building a business that will eventually manage clients, sell services, or scale beyond a single brand — GoHighLevel's architecture is built for that future.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Kartra or GoHighLevel better for beginners?

Kartra has a gentler learning curve for solopreneurs selling digital products. GoHighLevel has more features to learn upfront, but its templates and snapshots help agencies get started quickly. Both offer onboarding resources, but expect a steeper ramp-up with GHL if you're new to CRM-style tools.

### Can I migrate from Kartra to GoHighLevel?

Yes. You can export your contact lists from Kartra and import them into GoHighLevel. Funnels and automations will need to be rebuilt, but GHL's snapshot system means you can clone proven setups and adapt them rather than starting from scratch.

### Does GoHighLevel replace Kartra completely?

For agencies and service businesses, yes — GoHighLevel covers everything Kartra does and adds CRM, SMS, voice, AI agents, and white-labeling. For pure course creators who need advanced membership tools and built-in video hosting, Kartra still has an edge that GHL hasn't fully matched yet.

### Which platform has better customer support?

Both offer live chat and knowledge bases. Kartra's support tends to be faster for billing and technical issues. GoHighLevel has a massive community (Facebook group with 50,000+ members) and active developer ecosystem, which means answers to common questions are often available before you even contact support.

### Can I use both Kartra and GoHighLevel together?

Technically yes — some businesses use Kartra for course delivery and GoHighLevel for CRM and client communication. But this introduces complexity and cost. For most businesses, picking one platform and committing to it produces better results than splitting workflows across two systems.
