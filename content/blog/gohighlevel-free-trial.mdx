## What to Test During Your Trial

14 days is enough time to evaluate GHL meaningfully — if you focus on the features most relevant to your use case. Here's what I'd prioritize:

### 1. Build One Automation Workflow (Day 1–2)

The workflow builder is the core of GHL. Build a simple lead follow-up sequence:
- Trigger: form submission or manual contact creation
- Action 1: send a welcome email (immediately)
- Action 2: wait 1 hour → send SMS
- Action 3: wait 24 hours → send follow-up email

This one test tells you more about whether GHL fits your workflow than anything else. The visual builder either clicks for you or it doesn't.

### 2. Set Up a Pipeline and CRM (Day 2–3)

Create a pipeline that reflects your actual sales stages. Add a few test contacts and move them through stages. This gives you a feel for the CRM's usability and how well it maps to your process.

### 3. Try the Calendar Booking (Day 3–4)

Set up your calendar availability, create a booking page, and try booking an appointment as if you were a client. Test whether the automated reminder triggers work (create a workflow triggered by appointment booking and verify it fires).

### 4. Build a Landing Page or Funnel (Day 5–7)

If you're using GHL to replace a funnel builder, spend time in the page editor. It's functional but not Webflow — evaluating it honestly means spending real time with it.

### 5. Test the Conversation Inbox (Day 7–10)

If SMS is important to your business, test the two-way conversation inbox. Send a test message from a personal phone to your GHL number and see how responses work inside the platform.

### 6. Explore Sub-Accounts (Day 10–14, Agencies Only)

If you're an agency, create a sub-account for a test client and explore the snapshot/template system. Build a workflow in one sub-account and see how easy it is to clone to another. This is the workflow that makes GHL compelling at the Unlimited tier.
